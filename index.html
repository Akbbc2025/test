Definitions:

  1. Atom:

    2. It is the smallest particle that takes part in chemical reaction.

  2. Subatomic particle:

    3. It is the smallest particle that make up an atom.

  3. Duplet state:

    4. It is the condition in which the atom achieves stability by having 2 electrons in the K shell.

  4. Octate state:

    5. It is the condition in which the atom achieves stability by having 8 electrons in its valance shell.

  5. Valence shell:

    6. The outermost shell

  6. Valence electron:

    7. The electrons in the valence shell

  7. Valency:

    8. The combining capacity of one atom with another atom is called valancy
    or
    It refers to the electrons that can be exchanged or shared

  8. Ion:

    9. When an atom or group of atoms gains or loses electrons from their valance shell, in such cases the atoms is converted ion. Such charged atoms are called ions. It has 2 types:

    • Positively charged(+) ions:

      • When an atom of an element loses an electron from its valance shell.

    • Negatively charged(-) ions:

      • When an atom of an element gains an electron from its valance shell.

  9. Chemical bond:

    10. It is a kind of force that has been applied to bind the atoms of elements to form a compound. It is of 2 types:

    • Electrovalent bond:

      • The bond formed by the transfer of electrons from valence shell of metal to valance shell of non-metal

    • Covalent bond:

      • The chemical bond formed between two or more homogeneous or heterogeneous non-metals by sharing an equal number of electrons.

  10. Molecule:

    11. It is the smallest particle of a compound

  11. Molecular formula:

    12. The group of symbols of atoms that represent molecules (red text means its less important)

  12. Atomic mass:

    13. The sum of protons (p+) and neutrons (n0) present in the nucleus.

  13. Molecular mass:

    14. The sum of atomic mass of atoms present in a molecule

  14. Radioactive emission:

    15. If the ratio of neutron (n0) and protons (p+) is more than one, the nucleus bacomes unstable. the instability increases with the atomic number. Such elements want to go to a stable state from unstable state. In this condition, these atoms emit energetic radiation, which is know as radioactive emssion

  15. Radioactive elements:

    16. The atoms that emit powerful alpha, beta and gamma radiations to achieve stability

  16. Nuclear fission>

    17. The process of splitting unstable atoms (nucleus) with high atomic number into smaller atoms (nucleus).

  17. Nuclear fusion:

    18. The process of formation of heavy nucleus
    e.g. Helium (He)
    by the combination of lighter elements like Hydrogen (H) in the presence of high pressure and heat is called nuclear fusion.

  1. Elements are pure substances
  2. The average diameter of an atom in approximately 10-10m
  3. An atom has 3 parts:
    • Proton (p+)
    • Electron (e-
    • Neutron (n0
  4. Formula to calculate configuation of atoms is 2n2
  5. Only the atoms Hydrogen (H), Helium (He), Lithium (Li), Berellium (Be) and Boron (B) are able to obtain duplet state
  6. The electrons that are stable are in the 18th group of the periodic table where, they are known as noble gasses
