Definitions:

  1. Atom:

    It is the smallest particle that takes part in chemical reaction.

  2. Duplet state:

    It is the condition in which the atom achieves stability by having 2 electrons in the K shell.

  3. Octate state:

    It is the condition in which the atom achieves stability by having 8 electrons in its valance shell.

  4. Valency:

    The combining acapacity of onw atom with another atom is called valancy

  5. Ion:

    When an atom or group of atoms gains or loses electrons from their valance shell, in such cases the atoms is converted ion. Such charged atoms are called ions. It has 2 types:

    • Positively charged(+) ions:

      When an atom of an element loses an electron from its valance shell.

    • Negatively charged(-) ions:

      When an atom of an element gains an electron from its valance shell.

  6. Chemical bond:

    It is a kind of force that has been applied to bind the atoms of elements to form a compound. It is of 2 types:

    • Electrovalent bond:

      The bond formed by the transfer of electrons from valence shell of metal to valance shell of non-metal

    • Covalent bond:

      The chemical bond formed between two or more homogeneous or heterogeneous non-metals by sharing an equal number of electrons.
